Technical Responsibilities

As a 5-level avionics technician, I am qualified to independently diagnose and repair all pilot-interface electrical systems on the F-15 platform. This includes fuel quantity indication, radar systems, multi-mode communications, engine instrumentation, chaff/flare dispensing equipment, helmet-mounted systems, and numerous cockpit controls. My work directly impacts pilot situational awareness and mission capability.

Troubleshooting Process

When pilots report discrepancies, I gather detailed information through structured debriefs, attempt to replicate issues on the ground, and analyze technical documentation to identify potential failure modes. Using wiring diagrams, data flow analysis, and test equipment, I systematically isolate root causes, perform component-level repairs or replacements, conduct operational checks to verify fixes, and document all work in official maintenance records before releasing aircraft back to service.

Safety & Accountability

Aviation maintenance demands absolute adherence to Foreign Object Debris (FOD) prevention and tool control procedures. I maintain meticulous inventory tracking, follow strict safety protocols, and ensure all work complies with technical orders and Air Force instructions. These habits translate directly to precision engineering work in civilian applications.

As a Staff Sergeant and senior technician, I now mentor junior airmen through their qualification training.
