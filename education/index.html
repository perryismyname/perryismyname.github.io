Global Formula Racing (GFR) - Powertrain Team
Active member of OSU's international formula racing team, which collaborates with Duale Hochschule Baden-Württemberg (DHBW) in Germany to design and build competition vehicles across nine time zones. Work on the powertrain team encompasses the complete electric drivetrain system—from battery integration to motor control. Responsibilities include designing and fabricating battery management systems (BMS), battery enclosures and mounting structures, high-voltage bus bars, custom wiring harnesses, motor cooling systems, and electrical interconnects. Projects involve CAD design, manufacturing planning, and hands-on assembly as the team develops autonomous electric race vehicles for Formula SAE competition. Gained experience in high-voltage safety protocols, PCB design principles, and low-voltage control system integration.

OSU Machine Shop Experience

Why Robotics & Control Systems?

The Robotics & Control Systems option combines mechanical design with electrical systems integration, automation, and intelligent control—exactly the skillset needed for modern manufacturing and mechatronics applications. This specialization aligns with my hands-on experience in avionics, CNC programming, and embedded systems development.

Academic Philosophy

Maintaining a 3.86 GPA while balancing military service, professional athletics (Disney on Ice), and technical club involvement required disciplined time management and genuine passion for engineering. I approach coursework not as boxes to check, but as foundations for practical application. Theoretical concepts become meaningful when applied to real projects—whether troubleshooting F-15 avionics, programming CNC mills, or designing battery cooling systems for formula racing.

Hands-On Learning

The most valuable education happens outside the classroom. Machine shop work taught me how designs translate to manufacturability. Formula racing showed me how theoretical thermodynamics governs real cooling system performance. Military avionics work demonstrated how electrical theory becomes troubleshooting practice. Each experience reinforces classroom learning and reveals gaps to address through further study.

Transfer Student Journey

Transferring to OSU with three associate degrees reflects a non-linear path to engineering. Starting in pre-med, pivoting during COVID, and committing fully to engineering required persistence and adaptability. Arriving at OSU with strong math/science foundations and diverse coursework background allowed me to focus on major-specific technical depth while bringing broader perspective to team projects.
